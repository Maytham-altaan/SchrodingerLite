Design novel kinase inhibitors targeting the ATP-binding pocket of human
Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor (EGFR, UniProt P00533) — specifically the
T790M/L858R double-mutant form that drives resistance to first-generation
TKIs in non-small-cell lung cancer.

Binding-site features to exploit:
  • The hinge region (Met793 backbone) — H-bond acceptor + donor possible.
  • The gatekeeper Met790 (mutated from Thr790) — bulky hydrophobic pocket,
    sensitive to steric clashes; small flexible groups preferred near it.
  • The DFG-in conformation Asp855 carboxylate — H-bond donor reaches it.
  • A solvent-exposed front pocket — solubilizing groups (morpholine, piperazine)
    tolerated.

Hard requirements:
  • Avoid the quinazoline-4-amine scaffold of gefitinib/erlotinib
    (we want novelty against first-gen TKIs).
  • Avoid the acrylamide warhead of osimertinib unless absolutely needed —
    reversible binders preferred for safety.
  • Must be selective vs wild-type EGFR (i.e., exploit T790M's bulkier
    pocket — molecules should have a feature that fits 790M but clashes
    with smaller Thr790).
  • Oral-druggable: MW 350-500, LogP 2-4, TPSA < 120 Å², HBD ≤ 3.

Inspiration corner — known actives whose scaffolds you should NOT copy:
osimertinib, rociletinib, olmutinib, naquotinib. Invent something different.
